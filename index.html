General Information

University origin, ordinance, and academic structure

Origin & History

The University has its origin in three post-secondary colleges: New Asia College (founded 1949), Chung Chi College (founded 1951), and The United College of Hong Kong (founded 1956)...

The Ordinance

In November 1975, the Chancellor appointed an external commission to review constitutional arrangements. The second Fulton Report was submitted in March 1976...

Academic Structure

CUHK is a comprehensive research university with 8 Faculties, 60 departments/schools offering 85 major programmes and 76 minor programmes...

Undergraduate Studies

The University provides a wide variety of programmes to meet changing societal needs. In 2024-25, one major and three minor programmes are introduced...

Postgraduate Studies

149 master's programmes, 26 doctoral programmes, 44 MPhil-PhD programmes, plus postgraduate diploma and certificate programmes...

General Education

General Education equips students with intellectual capacity for understanding critical issues and prepares them as lifelong learners...

Fees & Colleges

College information, fees, scholarships, and student support

New Asia College

Founded in 1949 by Mr. Ch'ien Mu, promoting traditional Chinese culture with Western academics. Currently around 3,500 students...

United College

With motto "Make One's Virtues Shine and Renew the People", emphasizing whole-person education and global perspective...

Shaw College

Established in 1986 through Sir Run Run Shaw's generosity, with motto "Xiude Jiangxue" - cultivation of virtue and pursuit of knowledge...

Morningside College

Established in 2006, fully residential accommodating 300 students with diverse international community...

S.H. Ho College

Founded in 2006, accommodating 600 students on fully residential basis with motto "Culture, Morals, Devotion and Trustworthiness"...
